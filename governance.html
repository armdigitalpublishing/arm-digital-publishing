What We Do

The verified human oversight layer for AI.

AI is moving faster than the governance frameworks designed to contain it. In high-stakes environments — clinical settings, institutional decision-making, professional practice — this creates a specific and compounding risk: the tool makes a consequential decision, no human signed off on it, and nobody can prove otherwise.

ARM AI Governance builds the infrastructure that closes this gap — producing the documented proof of human accountability that regulators are beginning to require, insurers are beginning to price, and courts are beginning to hear cases about.

Why It Matters

The governance gap is real and it is widening.

01
The Liability Gap
Most organisations currently have no documented proof that a human reviewed their AI's consequential outputs. The liability sits with the developer, the practitioner, and the institution — often all three simultaneously.
02
The Regulatory Shift
Regulators are beginning to formalise what human oversight actually requires. The organisations that built their governance infrastructure early will be protected. Those that didn't will be exposed.
03
The Market Signal
Third-party certification is becoming a market access requirement — not just a compliance obligation. Health systems, insurers, and enterprise buyers increasingly require it before deployment.
How We Work

Three layers of verified oversight.

ARM AI Governance operates through a structured certification architecture — each layer adding a distinct form of verifiable human accountability to the AI systems we review.

Layer 01 — Intelligence
Regulatory Intelligence
Continuous monitoring of the AI governance landscape — regulatory developments, compliance requirements, and emerging standards. Published as an ongoing intelligence service for organisations operating in high-stakes environments.
Layer 02 — Verification
The Verified Report
A complete compliance document for the AI system or workflow under review — clinical logic audit, regulatory alignment assessment, bias review, and a clear record of every human decision point in the process. Designed to withstand scrutiny from a regulator, an insurer, or a judge.
Layer 03 — Certification
Living Certification
Ongoing certification maintenance — continuously updated as the regulatory environment evolves and as the certified system changes. Not a one-time award. A living status that requires ongoing compliance to maintain.
